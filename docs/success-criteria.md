# Success Criteria - BrabantHack_26 IKNL Med Tech Track

A solution is successful if it clearly demonstrates how reliable cancer information can be made more accessible, better connected, or easier to navigate through an innovative and technically meaningful approach. It should also show the value of AI based techniques, even for people who are not familiar with AI.

## Core KPI

The prototype must meaningfully improve the accessibility and connectedness of IKNL's trusted information sources (e.g., by linking, structuring, retrieving, visualizing, or contextualizing content).

---

## Domain 1: Information Integrity / Correctness

| # | Criterion |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | The solution actually provides an answer |
| 2 | Clearly show source provenance and reliability |
| 3 | Only use trusted IKNL sources |
| 4 | Avoid inventing, altering, or distorting medical information |
| 5 | Declines or redirects when it cannot provide an accurate answer |

## Domain 2: Usability

| # | Criterion |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | Creates better pathways to the right information for different target groups |
| 2 | Aligns with how people nowadays seek information |
| 3 | Helps users reach relevant information faster or with fewer steps compared to current IKNL websites (e.g., fewer clicks, smarter navigation, improved search, clearer summaries) |

## Domain 3: Ethics

| # | Criterion |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | Declines to answer in case of ethical issues |

## Domain 4: Advanced Solution

| # | Criterion |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | Connects our existing sources |
| 2 | The output creatively improves how users understand information and its context |
| 3 | Demonstrates potential for future implementation or further exploration within IKNL |

## Bonus

- Includes a simple mechanism for collecting user feedback on missing or unclear information

---

## Additional Value for IKNL

A solution is considered especially valuable if it:

- Helps IKNL understand new techniques, tools, or approaches for connecting or presenting trusted cancer information
- Is demo-able and technically insightful so that it can be shown to IKNL's MT and colleagues to spark discussion and understanding
- Can demonstrate potential for future implementation or further exploration within IKNL
- Offers trust in the use of new (AI) technologies for the general public good
- Includes a simple mechanism for collecting user feedback on missing or unclear information

## Target Groups

IKNL serves a wide range of target groups with very different needs:
- Political and regulatory stakeholders (VWS, RIVM, ZiNL)
- The general public
- Patients and their loved ones
- Healthcare professionals
- Partner/umbrella organizations such as hospitals and insurers
